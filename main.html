Products for HTML5 Beginners

This website is a toy example, filled with fake products for students to practice their HTML skills.

In part 1, students practice adding media (images, videos, forms) to this website.

In part 2, they will practice responsive design.

How it Works

Students must select tasks from a spreadsheet of options for each part.

They are scored out of up to 15 points worth of tasks, though they only need to complete 10 points perfectly to get full credit.

The other points come from participating in their group role, attending group meetings, and of course, their documentation.

Students are to use Git/GitHub to complete the assignment to track exactly who did what and to get used to a tool that is often used in industry.

Made For You

Students are welcome to adapt or change the website as they work on their tasks, though they will only be graded on the tasks that were assigned.

If students want to create their own tasks, they must ask for approval from the instructor.

Students may not make changes that would make a task easier. For example, they cannot reduce the number of "products" available or remove sections of text.

Start Your Journey Now

This project is a group project, since most computer science related jobs involve working with others. If not teammates, then clients.

Each student will still be graded individually, both on their work and on their participation as a group member.

The goal is to mimic a job environment. While most places don't have people who only know HTML/CSS, the people who do will often work on a team with people who also know HTML/CSS. Jobs are also typically done on pre-existing websites, which is why students will start with this one.

The other benefit of a group project is that ideally, most or all of the tasks are complete, showing a website that looks better than it started.
